1Definition of Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by either Party that is designated as confidential or that should reasonably be considered confidential given the nature of the information and circumstances of disclosure.

Examples of Confidential Information Include:

  • Technical specifications, system architectures, and infrastructure details
  • Business plans, strategies, and financial information
  • Customer lists, vendor relationships, and pricing information
  • Security protocols, vulnerabilities, and assessment results
  • Proprietary methodologies, processes, and know-how
  • Any information marked or designated as "Confidential"

2Confidentiality Obligations

The receiving Party shall:

Core Obligations

  • Limited Use: Use the Confidential Information solely for the agreed purpose as specified in this Agreement
  • Restricted Disclosure: Limit disclosure to employees, contractors, and advisors with a legitimate need to know who are bound by similar confidentiality obligations
  • Reasonable Care: Protect the Confidential Information using the same degree of care used to protect its own confidential information, but no less than reasonable care
  • Return/Destruction: Upon request or termination of discussions, promptly return or destroy all Confidential Information

3Exclusions from Confidentiality

The confidentiality obligations set forth in this Agreement do not apply to information that:

Information Excluded from Protection

  • Is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement
  • Is rightfully received from a third party without duty of confidentiality
  • Is independently developed without use of or reference to Confidential Information
  • Is required to be disclosed by law, court order, or government regulation (with prior notice to disclosing party when legally permissible)
  • Was known to the receiving party prior to disclosure, as evidenced by written records

4Term and Duration

Agreement Duration

  • Effective Period: This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues for three (3) years
  • Survival: Confidentiality obligations survive for three (3) years after termination of this Agreement
  • Trade Secrets: Trade secrets remain protected as long as they qualify as trade secrets under applicable law
  • Termination: Either party may terminate this Agreement with thirty (30) days written notice

5No Grant of Rights

No rights or licenses are granted by either Party under any patents, copyrights, trademarks, or other intellectual property rights except as expressly set forth in a separate written agreement between the parties.

Important Clarifications

  • This Agreement does not create a partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship
  • No obligation to enter into future business relationships
  • No warranty regarding the accuracy or completeness of Confidential Information

6Return or Destruction of Information

Upon written request by the disclosing Party, or upon termination of discussions, the receiving Party will promptly:

Required Actions

  • Return all documents and materials containing Confidential Information
  • Destroy all copies, notes, and derivative works containing Confidential Information
  • Provide written certification of such return or destruction upon request
  • Exception: Routine archival backups and information required to be retained by law may be retained subject to continued confidentiality obligations

7Remedies and Enforcement

The parties acknowledge that unauthorized use or disclosure of Confidential Information may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages would be inadequate.

Available Remedies

  • Injunctive Relief: The disclosing Party is entitled to seek immediate injunctive relief
  • Monetary Damages: Recovery of actual damages and any profits derived from unauthorized use
  • Attorney's Fees: Prevailing party may recover reasonable attorney's fees and costs
  • Cumulative Remedies: All remedies are cumulative and not exclusive

8General Provisions

Agreement Terms

  • Governing Law: This Agreement is governed by the laws of the state specified above
  • Entire Agreement: Constitutes the entire agreement regarding confidentiality between the parties
  • Amendments: Must be in writing and signed by both parties
  • Electronic Signatures: Electronic signatures are acceptable and legally binding
  • Severability: If any provision is unenforceable, the remainder remains in effect
  • Assignment: May not be assigned without prior written consent
