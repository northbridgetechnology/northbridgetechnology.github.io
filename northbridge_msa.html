This Master Services Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into by and between NorthBridge Technology ("Consultant") and the undersigned client ("Client"). This Agreement sets forth the terms and conditions governing the provision of professional IT consulting services by Consultant to Client.

Individual Statements of Work ("SOWs") executed by the parties will reference and incorporate this Agreement.

1Services & SOWs

Consultant will provide services as described in one or more SOWs executed by the parties. Each SOW will specify scope, deliverables, timeline, fees, and assumptions. If a conflict exists, the SOW controls for that engagement.

2Term & Termination

This Agreement is effective as of the Effective Date and remains in effect until terminated by either party upon thirty (30) days' written notice. Either party may terminate immediately for material breach not cured within ten (10) days of written notice. Upon termination, Client will pay for services rendered and approved expenses incurred through the termination date.

3Fees, Invoicing & Taxes

Fees will be as set forth in each SOW. Unless otherwise stated, invoices are due net thirty (30) days from date of invoice. Late payments may accrue interest at 1.5% per month or the maximum permitted by law. Client is responsible for applicable sales, use, or similar taxes (excluding taxes on Consultant's income).

4Change Management

Out-of-scope requests will be documented via a written Change Order signed by both parties prior to performance. Fees and schedule may be adjusted accordingly.

5Confidentiality

Each party agrees to keep confidential and not disclose to any third party any non-public information disclosed by the other party that is marked or reasonably understood to be confidential ("Confidential Information"). Obligations do not apply to information that is publicly available, rightfully received from a third party, independently developed without use of Confidential Information, or required to be disclosed by law.

6Data Security

Consultant will use commercially reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect Client data processed in connection with services. Consultant does not assume the role of data controller for Client data and will act as a processor where applicable.

7Intellectual Property

Pre-existing IP of each party remains that party's property. Unless otherwise stated in the SOW, Consultant grants Client a perpetual, non-exclusive, royalty-free license to use deliverables for Client's internal business purposes. Consultant may use generalized know-how and non-confidential learnings.

8Warranties & Disclaimers

Consultant represents it will perform services in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with industry standards.

EXCEPT AS EXPRESSLY SET FORTH, CONSULTANT DISCLAIMS ALL OTHER WARRANTIES, INCLUDING IMPLIED WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY, FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE, AND NON-INFRINGEMENT.

9Limitation of Liability

TO THE MAXIMUM EXTENT PERMITTED BY LAW, NEITHER PARTY WILL BE LIABLE FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, OR LOST PROFITS. CONSULTANT'S TOTAL LIABILITY ARISING OUT OF OR RELATED TO THIS AGREEMENT SHALL NOT EXCEED THE AMOUNTS PAID BY CLIENT UNDER THE APPLICABLE SOW IN THE SIX (6) MONTHS PRECEDING THE CLAIM.

10Non-Solicitation

During the term of any SOW and for twelve (12) months thereafter, neither party will knowingly solicit for employment any employee of the other party directly involved in the services, without prior written consent.

11Independent Contractor

Consultant is an independent contractor and not an employee, agent, joint venturer, or partner of Client. Consultant will be solely responsible for taxes, benefits, and insurance for its personnel.

12Insurance

Consultant maintains commercially reasonable insurance, which may include professional liability (E&O), general liability, and cyber liability insurance.

13Governing Law; Dispute Resolution

This Agreement will be governed by the laws of the state specified in the SOW, without regard to conflicts of laws. The parties will attempt good-faith negotiation; if unresolved, disputes may be submitted to mediation before litigation.

14Miscellaneous

This Agreement, together with SOWs, constitutes the entire agreement. Amendments must be in writing. Neither party may assign without consent, except to an affiliate or in connection with a merger or sale of substantially all assets.
